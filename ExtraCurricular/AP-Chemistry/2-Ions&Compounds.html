Ions

A normal Carbon Atom has:

  • 6 Protons
  • 6 Neutrons
  • 6 Electrons

This carbon atom is NEUTRAL

However when the charges are not neutral it is called an

Ion!

An Ion has two types:

  • Cation --> Positive
  • Anion --> Negative

12C- would be called a Carbon Anion 12C+ would be called a Carbon Cation

Naming Ions and Ionic Compounds

  • Groups with low numbers lose electrons
  • Groups with high numbers gain electrons

When a Potassium Cation + Chlorine Anion (K+Cl-) bonds, it's called:

Potassium Chloride

IMPORTANT: You add -ide for ALL the anions (You don't add anything for Potassium since we know it exchanges bonds)

Covalent Bonds: Molecule

Formed when two atoms share electron.

--> Forms a Molecule and can be represented by:

Chemical Formulas

C2H4O2

A molecule of Acetic acid:

  • 2 Carbon
  • 4 Hydrogen
  • 2 Oxygen

However it could also be written as: CH3COOH

Ions and Ionic Bond

Atoms ---> Ions | Gains or Lose Charge

In an Ionic Bond they don't share electrons:

  • One atom will take the other's electron
  • One --> Postive
  • 2nd --> Negative

Results in a strong attraction
